The problem

The world changes.
Most AI does not.

Today's AI systems are trained on fixed datasets and deployed into a world that keeps moving. When conditions shift — a patient's state, a climate pattern, a market regime — they struggle. They don't know what situation they're in.

01

No situational awareness

Standard models cannot detect when the world around them has fundamentally changed. They keep predicting as if nothing happened.

02

Retraining is not adaptation

Retraining takes time, data, and compute. Real-world systems need to adapt continuously — in the moment, without stopping.

03

No causal understanding

Pattern matching is not understanding. Without a causal model of the world, AI cannot distinguish what matters from what is noise.

04

Memory without structure

Current memory systems store sequences but cannot reason about what caused what — or what the current situation implies for the future.

Flagship Architecture — HCASFE-Net

Hierarchical Causal Associative
Sparse Free-Energy Network

HCASFE-net combines hierarchical temporal memory, online causal discovery, and adaptive surprise detection into a single unified architecture. It learns continuously without retraining, identifies regime changes in real time, and maintains episodic memory across interference.

Unlike gradient-descent systems that require backpropagation through time, HCASFE-net uses local predictive coding rules — the same learning principle believed to underlie biological intelligence. It runs entirely in pure NumPy, with no deep learning framework required.

Validated across multiple environments with genuinely different signal types. Generalisation confirmed. Results reproducible across random seeds.
